<img alt="Pipedream Gave Lleverage the Fast Lane to SaaS Integration Coverage" src="https://pipedream.com/blog/content/images/2025/05/Lleverage-logo-7.png" /><p><a href="https://www.linkedin.com/in/badr-eddial-63b84272?ref=pipedream.com"><strong>Badr Eddial</strong></a><br />Founder &amp; CTO at <a href="https://lleverage.ai/?ref=pipedream.com">Lleverage.ai</a></p><h3 id="about-lleverage"><strong>About Lleverage</strong></h3><p><a href="https://www.lleverage.ai/?ref=pipedream.com">Lleverage</a> is an AI workflow automation platform that helps businesses streamline and scale their core operations. It enables teams to combine AI, UI components, data pipelines, and third-party app integrations into unified, programmable workflows. Whether it&#x2019;s an AI-powered co-pilot working alongside a human or a fully autonomous background automation, Lleverage gives companies the building blocks to operationalize their most critical business processes.</p><p>For Lleverage, integrations aren&#x2019;t just a feature &#x2014; they&#x2019;re foundational. Customers expect their workflows to connect seamlessly with the tools they already use. By integrating with these systems, Lleverage can extract data, trigger actions, and deliver results back into their customers&apos; ecosystems, becoming a true extension of how work gets done.</p><h3 id="the-problem"><strong>The Problem</strong></h3><p>From day one, integrations were the top feature request from Lleverage customers. But building and maintaining these integrations in-house quickly became a drain on the engineering team. The effort to scope, build, and manage authentication, as well as navigate app review processes, constantly pulled the engineering team away from working on the core product..</p><p>As a startup, investing a year or more of engineering effort into building the top 50&#x2013;100 SaaS integrations would come at a massive opportunity cost. Lleverage explored other embedded integration platforms, but these required too much custom development and configuration; often involving going through each app&#x2019;s approval process themselves. They needed a faster, more scalable path to go to market with integrations.</p><h3 id="the-solution"><strong>The Solution</strong></h3><p>Lleverage adopted <a href="https://pipedream.com/connect?ref=pipedream.com"><strong>Pipedream Connect</strong></a> to power their integrations and quickly bring the top 50&#x2013;100 SaaS apps into their platform. Within just a few days, they had a proof of concept live. And after a few more weeks of polishing the production implementation, they were able to launch a fully integrated experience to their users.</p><p>Pipedream handled some of the hardest parts of delivering integrations: managing complex authentication flows, maintaining reliable triggers and actions, and offering out-of-the-box support for thousands of SaaS applications. Instead of investing months into building auth and API connectors themselves, Lleverage was able to plug into Pipedream&#x2019;s platform and go live with a broad library of integrations.</p><blockquote><strong>&#x201c;We likely saved about 1 to 2 years of effort, let alone the opportunity cost for a startup like ours.&#x201d;</strong> &#x2014; Badr Eddial, CTO at Lleverage</blockquote><p>Today, all of their most-requested integrations are available and seamlessly embedded into Lleverage&#x2019;s AI-powered co-pilot. Users can log in, describe what they want to automate in natural language, and the platform rolls out the automation, powered by Pipedream behind the scenes.</p>
